# SOURCES — Foreign Geologist Films, Investigation Pass 1
Companion to `geologist_films_screening_pass1.csv`. Extends Sturkell et al. (*Geology Today*, 2026).

Sources are grouped by film. These are the references actually consulted during the
initial eligibility/character pass. **All character fates, on-screen terminology, and
"VERIFY" items still require confirmation on viewing** — database synopses and reviews
are starting points, not final codes.

Reference tiers used: primary/authoritative (Criterion, Wikipedia, national film
archives, festival/distributor pages) > database aggregators (TMDB, IMDb, Letterboxd,
FilmAffinity) > review/blog (used only for plot/fate detail, flagged as such).

## GENERAL / FRAMEWORK SOURCES
- Sturkell, E., Sjöqvist, A., Björklund, L. & Johnsson, A. (2026). "Geologists on the silver screen—the sequel." *Geology Today*, 42(2), 82–86. DOI: 10.1111/gto.70014 (supplied PDF) — death taxonomy, demographic baselines, eligibility definition adopted/extended here.
- Thaler, P. (2026, 15 May). "Murder, monsters, occupational hazards: Why movie geologists die so often." *Science* (AAAS). DOI: 10.1126/science.zzqrf63 (supplied PDF) — popular summary of the above.
- Source data: `geologist_foreign_films.csv` (TMDB pull, keyword 161281 "geologist"; script `tmdb_geologist_foreign.py`).

## CAVEATS
- TMDB/IMDb/Letterboxd synopses frequently blur geologist vs geophysicist/engineer/archaeologist — every "geologist_explicitly_named_on_screen = Y" still needs on-viewing confirmation of the exact on-screen term, per Sturkell's own geologist-vs-geoscientist distinction.
- Grokipedia entries were used only for corroboration of plot points already attested elsewhere, never as sole source.
- Character fates marked "?" or "VERIFY" in the CSV are explicitly NOT settled by these sources.
